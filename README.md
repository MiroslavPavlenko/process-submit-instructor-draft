## Purpose

Graduating with a computer science or software engineering degree is the minimum need for studios like Scopely, who hire applicants who can demonstrate real-world shipping work before walking across the stage. This guide outlines the best practices to follow between now and graduation to ensure that your portfolio, abilities, and professional presence reflect your development as a full-stack game engineer. Scopely's full-stack engineer position necessitates Unity and C# skills, backend API knowledge, and familiarity with cloud platforms, all of which a degree does not guarantee.[^1] Following these procedures puts you ahead of every contender whose GitHub is empty and whose résumé solely has coursework. Your recruiter is not reading your transcript; instead, he or she is reading your projects.

## Best Practices

### Build Real Projects Outside of Class

Course projects tell a recruiter you can follow instructions; personal projects tell them you can think independently and ship something real. Participate in at least two game jams per year on itch.io, publish every project to a public GitHub repository with a descriptive README, and treat each one as a portfolio piece not a throwaway weekend exercise.

### Sharpen Your Technical Interview Skills Early

Game studio technical rounds test data structures, algorithms, and problem-solving under pressure, and waiting until senior year to start practicing is one of the most common reasons otherwise strong candidates do not make it past the first round. Solve at least three LeetCode problems per week starting in your sophomore year, prioritize medium-difficulty problems in arrays, graphs, and dynamic programming, and time yourself to simulate real interview conditions.

### Make Your Online Presence Work for You

A [LinkedIn](www.linkedin.com) profile with a blank experience section and a GitHub with no recent commits signals to a recruiter that you are not actively building which is the opposite of what a live service game studio wants to see. Pin your two strongest Unity or backend projects to your GitHub profile, keep your commit history active with consistent weekly pushes, and write a LinkedIn headline that names your target role and your core skills rather than just listing your degree.

### Get Hands-On With Cloud Platforms

Live-service games like Pokémon GO run on cloud infrastructure, and a fullstack role at Scopely explicitly expects exposure to [GCP](https://docs.cloud.google.com/api-gateway/docs), [AWS](https://aws.amazon.com/api-gateway/), or Azure before your first day.[^1] Complete one personal cloud project each semester on a free tier account deploy a [REST API](https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/rest-apis), connect it to a database, document the architecture, and add it to your portfolio. Knowing how to spin up and tear down cloud services is the difference between a candidate who needs onboarding and one who can contribute on day one.

### Target a Game Studio Internship Before Senior Year

An internship at a game studio even in a role adjacent to your target — signals that you understand the production pace, cross-functional collaboration, and ship it culture that separates game development from standard enterprise software work. Start applying to summer internships in October of your sophomore year, because most major studios, including Scopely's parent companies, open applications six to eight months before the start date.

## Learning Roadmap

### Mapping the Plan Across Your Remaining Semesters

Treating these five practices as things to do "eventually" is how engineers reach senior year with a resume full of class projects and nothing else. The figure below maps each practice to the academic year where starting it has the highest return because the compounding effect of two years of consistent GitHub activity is something you cannot replicate in a single semester.

**Figure 1. Fullstack Game Engineer Skill Building Timeline by Academic Year**

By sophomore year you are shipping game jam projects and solving [LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/problemset/) weekly. By junior year you have an internship on your resume and a cloud project in your portfolio. By senior fall you are polishing, not building from scratch which is exactly where you want to be when recruiters start reviewing applications.


### Staying on Track

Set a calendar reminder each November and April to review your GitHub activity, count your shipped projects, and update your LinkedIn with anything new. Falling behind in sophomore year is recoverable; falling behind in the semester before graduation is not.
